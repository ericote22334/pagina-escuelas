About La Costa

La Costa is a coastal district in the Province of Buenos Aires, Argentina. Known for its extensive beaches and tourist-friendly towns, it stretches over 90 kilometers along the Atlantic Ocean. It is a popular destination for summer vacations and offers a mix of nature, recreation, and local culture.

The region is made up of various towns, including San Clemente del Tuyú, Santa Teresita, Mar del Tuyú, San Bernardo, Mar de Ajó, and others. Each has its own unique attractions, such as aquariums, natural reserves, piers, and seasonal festivities that draw visitors from across the country.

In addition to tourism, La Costa supports a year-round population with essential infrastructure such as hospitals, schools, public transport, and municipal services. It is governed by a local municipality and continues to develop as both a tourist hub and a residential area.
